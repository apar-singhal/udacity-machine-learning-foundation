    return dataset[group_by].unique()

def get_groupby_mean(df, group_by, correlation_with):
    """
    Method to get the mean of 'correlation_with' columns when grouped by 'group_by' column
    """
    return df.groupby(group_by)[correlation_with].mean()

Conclusions¶

We analysed the 'No-show Appointments' dataset on Age, Neighbourhood and Scholarship.

  • We found some correlation b/w the age and showing up to the appointment. (Though we have not performed any statistical tests and hence cannot confirm this result)
  • We saw that in the given dataset, people from 'ILHA DO BOI' and 'PARQUE INDUSTRIAL' have the highest average probability of showing up on the appointment date.
  • We also found that for the given dataset, people without scholarship have around 4% more probablity of showing up than those with scholarship

PS: All the results above are tentative as we have not performed any statistical tests.

Limitations¶

It would have been better if there had been more relevant features in the datasets like below:

  • Distance of neighbourhoods from the clinic
  • Have these appointments being scheduled by the patient or by someone else on the behalf of patients
  • What is the overall review (rating) for the clinics. It is possible that patients would not come for appointment only in some particular clinics.

There are some other factors that we are not sure of, and which may affect the accuracy of our prediction:

  • We do not know about how easy it is to find the location of treatment. Maybe some people come for treatment but they were not able to find the location
  • We are also not sure if no-show covers the case when a patient may have arrived later than his original appointment time
  • What happens if a patient re-schedule his appointment. Was this data updated?
Hence we may not have a concrete solution since there could be many uncontrolled variables that could have resulted in the no-show scenario.¶
